Sanatorium Medea

Tskaltubo, Georgia

Beginning at Tskaltubo's bathhouse-filled parklands, there is a path that takes you across a main road and up an overgrown path. From between the trees, a grand pillared entrance gradually comes into view. Constructed in a Stalinist Neoclassical style, it blends monumental classicism with socialist ideals, into something that is often referred to as "Soviet Empire style". Yet behind this historical façade lies a rundown structure: cracking concrete, broken glass, decaying plaster, and the buzzing of bees who have made the arched blue ceiling their new home.

Officially named Hotel Medea, it is one of the many abandoned sanatoriums and hotels in Tskaltubo. Built between the years of 1954 and 1962, the building has a number of wings and a capacity for 326 beds.

Around nine thousand refugees from the 1992–1993 Georgia–Abkhazia conflict found temporary shelter in Tskaltubo’s vacant sanatoriums, including this one. Similar to Hotel Aia, there are still Internally Displaced Persons (IDPs) living in the abandoned Hotel Medea today.
