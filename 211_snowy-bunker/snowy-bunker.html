Alternative Command Bunker P

Somewhere, Germany

During the Cold War, the Ministry for State Security in East Germany (commonly known as the Stasi) built a secret underground bunkers across the GDR. Known as Ausweichführungsstellen (AFüSt), their purpose was to ensure the continuity of operations if the Cold War ever turned hot.

In the event of a nuclear strike, a conventional war, or civil unrest, the Stasi leadership would retreat to these bunkers to maintain their surveillance and intelligence apparatus. Almost every district in East Germany maintained its own bunker.

These bunkers were built under unassuming buildings or within forests to avoid detection, helping them remain secret to many lower-level police until after the fall of the Berlin Wall. Fully self-sufficient, the bunkers were equipped with air filtration, power generation, water wells, and enough provisions to sustain over 100 personnel.

This particular bunker was built in 1970 based on the 1/15/V2b template. It had two normal staircase entrances (now closed), four emergency ladder exits, and over twenty long rooms arranged in a grid layout. The exterior rooms once housed battery systems, air filtration, and water tanks, while the interior hermetically sealed rooms contained the communication, work, bunk, food preparation, and medical rooms.

I had the opportunity to explore this bunker on a bright, snowy day when one of the emergency exits was accessible. Descending through the exit's narrow opening felt like taking a step into the past. A yellow and orange relic created in preparation for a conflict that, fortunately, never happened.
