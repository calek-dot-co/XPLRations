Lenin's Garden

Malcoci, Moldova

A small open-air museum on the outskirts of Chișinău has become Lenin's new home. Homeless in Moldova since the 1991 fall of the Soviet Union, he now takes up residence here.

At first, Lenin was just alone with his toys on the grassy lawn. Decommissioned helicopters, old biplanes, and weathered rocket models. But soon, he was joined by more homeless Lenins.

They arrived from public squares, goverment buildings, and farming collectives across the country. Some stood tall and imposing in granite, while others were short, carved from marble, or cast in heavy bronze.

Together, they form an unusual brotherhood. Stripped of their pedestals and the power of their ideas, the old revolutionaries have entered their final, permanent exile in this open-air graveyard of Soviet history.
