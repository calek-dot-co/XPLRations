Partisan Hospital Memorial

Grmeč Mountain, Bosnia and Herzegovina

In late 1942, as Partisan forces began liberating parts of northwest Bosnia from Axis occupation, they succeeded in connecting these newly freed areas with other liberated territories in western Bosnia—forming what became known as the Bihać Republic. These early victories created the opportunity to establish a broader support network away from the front lines. On the slopes of Grmeč Mountain, five hospitals were constructed, the largest of which was located on the northern side. It was named Korčanica.

As the war progressed, the Korčanica hospital treated approximately 2,800 wounded and typhus patients before early 1943, when the Axis command launched Operation Case White, a major offensive aimed at reclaiming the Bihać Republic and capturing the Partisan leader, Josip Tito.

In honour of those who lost their lives on the slopes of Grmeč, local and regional authorities constructed the Monument to Korčanica on the original hospital site. Designed to resemble a budding flower, this spomenik symbolises the renewed freedom of the Bosnian people.

Memorial walk

Behind the main spomenik complex, a narrow paved path winds through the forest. Along this route, several smaller memorial sculptures mark the former locations of hospital buildings.

Each sculpture once featured an inscription, indicating the specific function of the building once located there, such as surgical ward, power station, kitchen, bakery, or bunks. While some show visible signs of vandalism, most have simply suffered from nearly five decades of neglect.
