Anakila Tower

Anakila, Georgia

Like something lifted from the set of a science fiction film, this tower of glass, metal, and fibreglass looms over the horizon of Georgia’s Black Sea coast. Initially intended as a lighthouse to help with maritime navigation, then planned as a visitors centre and restaurant, today, the building stands abandoned. Its foundations being slowly exposed by the shifting sands is the only sign of progress here.

Originally envisioned as an symbol of new development in Georgia’s north-west coastal region—near the border of Russian-occupied Abkhazia—the tower was one of the first constructions in the ambitious Lazika urban development project. Proposed in 2011 by then-President Mikheil Saakashvili, the project was shelved several years later when the new Georgian Dream government deemed it unfeasible and officially cancelled it.

The Lazika project imagined a bold new city: a home for 500,000 people built on reclaimed wetlands, anchored by a deep-sea port and bustling residential and commercial districts to rival Batumi. Unfortunatly this is a future we can only dream about while exploring this forgotten shell of a tower.
