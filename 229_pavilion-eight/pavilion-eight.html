Pavilion Number 8

Somewhere, Moldova

Starting with agricultural exhibitions in 1956, MoldExpo's 24 hectares of exhibition land are home to numerous modern buildings, the last remaining Lenin statue in Chișinău, and the wonderful abandoned Pavilion Number 8.

Sitting opposite the main outdoor exhibition area, it's not like this building is hidden. Its large, half-broken glass windows, its wide-open doors, and its metal crest all look out to a normal courtyard that is quite often hosting thousands of people. So why does the only international exhibition space in Chișinău have an abandoned building right in the middle of its space?

Unfortunately, a verifiable answer to that question remains slightly out of reach for me with my lack of Romanian language and local contacts. Some sources online say it's because of divided ownership, with the land being owned by the state enterprise and the building by a private company. Other online sources say that it's just not economical to restore now that modern exhibitions require tall ceilings, flexible layouts, and technological integration.

Either way, it's a shame this has been left to decay to this state. Its double glass wall structure gives the whole ceiling a floating feeling, like it's magically suspended, and in the centre of the room is what was probably once an amazing mosaic fountain centerpiece. As quality of life and public infrastructure continue to improve in Moldova, I hope this pavilion stops being neglected and is given a second chance at life.
