Rusting Mining Tower

Slovakia

From one side of a city to another, metal towers like this one once carried ore from a mine’s processing plant to the local train station. Each tower supported at least six cables: two for transporting the ore, and four suspending protective mesh to prevent rocks from falling on to the rivers, roads, walking tracks, and homes below.

When the local mine closed in 1999, the towers stopped being used. Most were left to rust, though a few found a second life as mobile phone reception towers. The tower I climbed is the tallest of them all, spanning from one valley to the next. Sixteen ladders ascend sixteen storeys to a small platform offering spectacular views of the city.

Of course, the visibility works both ways—which is why I made the climb under the cover of night, guided only by moonlight. In the darkness, the protective mesh appeared sturdy enough to support a person's weight—but stepping onto it felt like a risk too far, even for me.
