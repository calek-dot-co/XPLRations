Memorial Graveyard

Lepoglava, Croatia

Following the Axis invasion of Lepoglava in World War II, the Lepoglava prison (which had once detained communist activist Josip Tito in the 1930s) was transformed into a death camp operated by the ultra-nationalist and fascist Ustaše forces.

Approximately 5,000 individuals were killed through mass executions and inhumane conditions during the site's operation as the "Lepoglava Workers' Camp for Men, Women and Children". This spomenik commemorates those victims who lost their lives at the prison camp and the surrounding areas.

The geometric aluminium sculpture stands amidst white gravestone cubes, each bearing the names of victims murdered at this site, located on the slope of a small hill just outside the town. Although there is no official interpretation of the sculpture’s symbolism, I couldn’t help but feel that its highlights and shadows evoke the spirit and struggle of those who resisted the brutality of fascism.
