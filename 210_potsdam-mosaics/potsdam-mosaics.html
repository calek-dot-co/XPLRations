Mosaics of Contrast

Potsdam, Germany

Nestled within the Brandenburg woods, less than an hour from Berlin, lies Potsdam. Once part of the GDR (East Germany) and the Soviet Union, it now looks towards the west for its future, drawing visitors primarily to the grandeur of Sanssouci Palace and Babelsberg Park. However, I visited to view a pair of very different mosaics.

Cosmonaut of Potsdam

Wrapping around two sides of the former Datenverarbeitungszentrum (data centre) building in Potsdam are eighteen mosaic panels. These artworks illustrate the evolution of scientific progress, featuring Yuri Gagarin, cosmic exploration, landmark breakthroughs, and a quote by Karl Marx.

By Fritz Eisel, 1971. Location: 52.395700, 13.052978

The mosaic text is a quote from Karl Marx, from his notebooks known as the "Grundrisse" (Foundations of the Critique of Political Economy), written around 1858. It captures one of his most interesting ideas: wealth isn't about money, it’s about time.

“The less time society requires to produce wheat, cattle, etc., the more time it wins for other production, material or intellectual. Economy of time—to this, all economy ultimately reduces itself. Economy of time, as well as the planned distribution of labor time among the various branches of production, remains the first economic law on the basis of social production.”

Adam and Eve

Covering a whole end-wall of an apartment building in Potsdam is this large 25 x 12 metre mosaic depicting a man and a woman at the start of the world. The apple and the snake are not represented here, but a burning bush and a bird representing the Holy Spirit are. A interesting depiction of religion from the east.

By Peter Rohn, 1981. Location: 52.394810, 13.041252
