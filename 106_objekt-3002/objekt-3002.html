Somewhere, Poland

While travelling through Eastern Europe, I've repeatedly found myself drawn to Monolith bunkers. With twelve of these sites scattered across five countries, it's likely you'll find one nearby if you're anywhere close to the former Iron Curtain border.

The condition of these abandoned bunkers ranges widely. Just this past weekend, I visited what I believe to be both the best-preserved and the most deteriorated examples in a single weekend. The following is my visit to one of the worst.

Bunker East-West

Accessible through narrow crawl spaces between concrete blocks, the interior reveals the burnt-out shell of a former nuclear weapons storage bunker.

Fortunately, the weapons were never launched—but if they had been, this is how I imagine the world would look: scorched paint, rusting metal, and rubble scattered everywhere.
