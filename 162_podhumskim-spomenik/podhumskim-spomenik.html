Monument to the Victims of Fascism

Podhum, Croatia

Behind a beautifully made circular stone wall in Podhum stands a 22-metre-high memorial obelisk. Near the obelisk, set into the stone wall, is a white plaque whose translation from Croatian to English reads:

"At this place, on the 12th of July 1942, fascist occupiers brutally executed 91 patriots aged between 16 and 60 years from the village of Podhum, part of the legendary Grobnik Field. These bright graves will tell future generations about the immeasurable sacrifices and the heroic deeds of the sons and daughters of the Croatian coastland, Istria, and Gorski Kotar. They will speak of the days of the glorious and victorious four-year people’s liberation struggle, fought for a new life in freedom, for the reunification of Istria with the Croatian homeland, and for the brotherly community of the peoples of socialist Yugoslavia."
