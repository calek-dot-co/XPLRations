Chronicles of Georgia

Tbisili, Georgia

In the northern hills of Tbilisi sits a gigantic monument. Made from bronze, copper and stone, its 16 columns, each 35 metres high, cast dramatic shadows as you walk among them. Across three levels of panels, each pillar of the Chronicles of Georgia depicts stories, history, and tradition.

The lowest level tells the story of the Bible, the flood, the Last Supper, and the teachings of Jesus. The middle level preserves Georgia's rich history, from kings and artists to Saint Nino, who introduced Christianity to Georgia, to the country's annexation by the Russian Empire in 1801. The top level honours cultural traditions, early evidence of winemaking to Soviet-era astrological symbols.

No other monument in Georgia so powerfully captures its three millennia of statehood and two millennia of Christian history.
