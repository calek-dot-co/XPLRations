Monument to the Vođenica Company

Vođenica, Bosnia and Herzegovina

Hidden among the trees stand four concrete pillars, each facing each other. Their box-like form evokes the image of an unexploded World War II bomb, with its tail jutting up from the earth. This unusual spomenik, located along a dirt road in rural Bosnia and Herzegovina, honours the fighters of the Vođenica Company who sacrificed their lives in the struggle against fascism.

Composed of residents from nearby villages, the Vođenica Company began by sabotaging communication lines and ambushing convoys travelling between Krnjeuša and Bosanski Petrovac. As their numbers grew to over 100 members, they joined the Krajina Partisan Brigade in November 1942 to help briefly reclaim the city of Bihać from German occupation. By late 1944, they had succeeded in driving Axis forces out of their region of Bosnia and Herzegovina.
