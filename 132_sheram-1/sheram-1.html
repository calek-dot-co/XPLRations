Sheram I

Gyumri, Armenia

Scattered across the landscape near Gyumri, Armenia, large pre-fabricated shells dot the landscape. If you head north-west a few kilometres, you'll see the suburbs of Sheram I and Sheram II rising from the barren, unfinished ground. These developments were originally intended to house Armenians displaced by the devastating 1988 earthquake, but construction came to a halt following the collapse of the Soviet Union.

For nearly thirty years, the area has remained largely abandoned. Some of the incomplete structures now shelter internally displaced Armenians, while others slowly deteriorate under the elements.
