Ledena Pecina Ice Cave Hike

Durmitor National Park, Montenegro

Play audio

The original plan for this mid-autumn day was to hike to the summit of Bobotov Kuk, the tallest mountain in Montenegro. However, upon arriving on the southern side of Durmitor National Park, we were met with bleak conditions—howling winds, freezing temperatures, and frost covering everything. It was my second attempt in as many years to reach the summit, and once again, bad weather stood in the way.

Fortunately, we had a backup plan that better matched the local weather patterns. On the northern side of the mountain, conditions were much more favourable—calm winds, mild temperatures, and only a little snow. With a new goal in mind, we set off towards the Ledena Pećina Ice Cave.

The 14-kilometre hike climbs nearly 800 metres, passing the scenic Black Lake and many amazing viewpoints that look out across the high plains towards the Tara Canyon. But the true highlight lies at the end of the trail: a year-round ice cave filled with stalactites and stalagmites.

Even after a warm summer, ice covered the entrance slope into the cave. With the help of a dodgy wire and rope system, I entered down into the cave. Inside, the temperature dropped significantly but not enough to keep the cave fully frozen. Alongside the icy view was also a symphony of dripping water and thawing pools, which you can see and listen to on this page.
