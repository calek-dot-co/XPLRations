Former Archaeological Museum

Tbilisi, Georgia

Currently closed, but under constant police guard, the former Museum of Archaeology in Tbilisi overlooks the Kura River and the valley Tbilisi is nestled in. Clearly influenced by the artefacts it once housed, the cave-like entrance and front façade give the feeling of a modern brutalist tomb. Above the building's entrance is a large bas-relief depicting an exhumed body by sculptor Tengiz Kikalishvili. The museum is one of three circular structures embedded in the hillside; the other two are former water reservoirs for the local community.

Sharing the hillside is the nearby St. Nino Monument. One of Georgia's most celebrated religious figures, she preached a message that resulted in the Christianisation of the region of Georgia in the 300s AD. Construction of the monument began in 1988 when Georgia was part of the Soviet Union, a surprising approval considering the regime's typical anti-religious stance.
