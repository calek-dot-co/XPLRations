Soviet Relics of Blava

Bratislava, Slovakia

Located at the corner of Obchodná and Kollárovo streets in Bratislava, this bas-relief sculpture features soldiers flanking the Czechoslovak coat of arms. Created in the 1930s by Slovak modernist sculptor Ladislav Majerský, the piece reflects a hallmark of Soviet-era design: disproportionately large hands to emphasise the strength of the working class and the power of labor.

Location: 48.148991, 17.11357

While the first image might be mistaken for typical Soviet heroic realism, this nearby mosaic offers a more overtly propagandistic message. Depicting Sputnik and early space exploration, it celebrates the Soviet Union’s technological and scientific achievements in the late 1950s, specifically the launch of the first man-made satellite into Earth’s orbit. Despite its historic significance for the USSR, the event held little direct relevance to Czechoslovakia at the time.

Location: 48.151294, 17.112569

Located on the side of the Faculty of Architecture and Design building, this is one of nineteen bas-relief sculptures depicting everyday elements of Soviet-era life. This one represents precision industrial work and manual labour, idealised musculature (with more realistic hand proportions), and a sense of cooperation.

Location: 48.151010, 17.111615

Fontána Družba—known in English as the Union Fountain—sits at the centre of Freedom Square. Originally completed in the 1980s, the fountain was renovated in 2023 to be more inclusive and accessible, notably through the removal of low boundary walls designed to prevent children from entering the water.

Location: 48.152121, 17.111731

Finally, the Slovak Radio building stands as one of Bratislava’s most iconic landmarks. Conceived as a “temple of word and music,” this inverted pyramid contains a suspended inner pyramid, designed to improve acoustic insulation given its location near tramlines.

Location: 48.154067, 17.114093
