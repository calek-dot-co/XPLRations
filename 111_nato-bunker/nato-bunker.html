NATO Radar Bunker

Somewhere, Belgium

Thirty metres beneath a rural field in Belgium lies a former three-level command and control bunker, once used to monitor Belgian and Luxembourg airspace. Connected by underground cabling, it served as the data processing hub for information gathered by the above-ground radar.

Operated by a team of more than 200 people, this bilingual air force support unit was responsible for safeguarding the integrity of the airspace above—responding to situations ranging from lost communications and aircraft in distress, to suspicious flight activity over sensitive sites like nuclear power plants, or incidents involving illegal drone use.

Unlike its solid metal, above-ground counterpart, the bunker’s interior is vulnerable to vandalism. Hidden from public view, it's an easy target for graffiti, general destruction of paneling, and theft of copper. But no matter the damage people do, the bunker will continue to stand, the three stories of reinforced concrete will probably outlast us all.
