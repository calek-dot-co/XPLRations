Nutsubidze Skybridge

Tbilisi, Georgia

The idea of a skybridge, connecting a vast network of skyscrapers with pedestrian bridges, was inspired by the historial Georgian village of Shatili, known for its elevated walkways connecting clusters of homes.

This modern urban reinterpretation aimed to enhance comfort during hot summers, offer a practical alternative in icy winters, and strengthen the connection between residents and their communities. Skybridges would all but replace winding, steep, and narrow roads as part of a new utopian urbanism vision.

Designed by Georgian architects Otar Kalandarishvili and Gizo Potskhishvili, the first part of this vision was constructed between 1974 and 1978 in a hilly part of western Tbilisi. Unfortunatly, due to budget constraints, the project was only partially completed, resulting in the construction of only a single bridge connecting three residential buildings.

Renovated in early 2025, the Nutsubidze skybridge remains in use today as a public walkway. A pay-as-you-go elevator provides access to its southern end, from which you can move freely between the connected buildings.
