Old Jewish Cemetery

Chișinău, Moldova

At its peak in 1903, Chișinău's population was 46% Jewish. Today, the estimated Jewish population for all of Moldova has dwindled to just 15,000.

One legacy of the former inhabitants can be found in the suburbs of Chișinău. Its an overgrown cemetery containing over 20,000 Jewish graves. While Hebrew script and the Star of David cover most of the headstones, Russia's historical connection to the land and its people remains visible in occasional Cyrillic inscription.

Navigating the seemingly endless grave-lined paths and avoiding packs of stray dogs makes for a fun explore, but the highlight of this visit is the abandoned Beit Kaddishim building. This historic funeral synagogue, where the deceased were washed before burial, survived shelling during World War II but unfortunately not decades of neglect. It now lies in ruins, with only its brick dome and dual Star of David doors left standing as a monument to the past.
