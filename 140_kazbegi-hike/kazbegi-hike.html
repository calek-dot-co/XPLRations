Kazbegi Hike

Stepantsminda, Georgia

Beginning in the valley town of Stepantsminda, the hiking trail winds up the mountain past the Gergeti Trinity Church and into the snow-covered terrain. This marks the first leg of the well-known Kazbegi hike in northern Georgia, near the border with Russia.

Roughly halfway through the hike, with the peak looming above, one of the mountain's six glaciers comes into view. The Gergeti Glacier stretches approximately seven kilometres down the southeastern slope and was my goal today.

Continuing up the mountain until I reached Altihut 3014—situated about two kilometres from the glacier—proved challenging, with knee-high snow still covering the trail during my early summer visit. Beyond that point, deeper snow, hidden crevasses, and the threat of avalanches made sure that I wasn't able to continue onto the glacier. Perhaps next time.
