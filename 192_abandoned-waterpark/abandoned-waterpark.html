Abandoned Water Park

Somewhere, Greece

From a drone's perspective, the layout of the abandoned water park is easy to make out. But once you're deep between the tubes and sides, the twists and turns take on a maze-like life of their own. Rising above the overgrown vegetation, each slide spirals around itself, weaves through others, or stretches off into the distance.

This water park has been shut down since 2014, when a gust of wind created a freak accident with an inflatable ball, killing one teenager and seriously injuring another. The park was swiftly closed and the resulting investigation determined 'bad weather conditions and human error' were at fault.

When I visited, over a decade later on a warm winter's day, the empty grounds were eerily silent. Only the occasional call of a bird interrupted the steady hum of the nearby freeway. With no security present, I was able to wandered freely up many slides and tubes, through circular bowls, and finally out onto the staircased towers. This strange reversal, starting at the bottom and ending at the top, perfectly captured the surreal empty vibe of this long-disused attraction.
