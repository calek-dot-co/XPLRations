Monument to the Detachment in Brozovica Forest

Novo Selo Palanječko, Croatia

This monument and accompanying plaque commemorate the first Partisan armed anti-fascist resistance unit formed in occupied Yugoslavia.

From the outside, the ten pillars appear as a unified structure. Together, they abstractly reference an elm tree that stood on this exact site on 22 June 1941, when the resistance unit first assembled. Once inside, its clear that the monument is hollow, symbolising both the absence of the former tree and the lives lost during the war.

The names shown on the plaque are the founding members: Antun Čavrak, Stjepan Drnić, Ivan Draženović, Đuro Djulaj, Ivan Ivanuš, Dragutin Kokot, Žarko Mihaljević, Stjepan Prša, Josip Rožanković, Stjepan Rožanković, Ivan "Rode" Rapić, and Branko Vuletić.
