Palace of Culture

Jerkum, Armenia

Named after the Armenian word for ‘warm mineral spring’, Jermuk is a former medical tourism town in southern Armenia.

From the 1960s until the collapse of the Soviet Union, the town was known for the healing qualities of its natural spring, making it an ideal site for sanatoriums: institutions dedicated to wellness and recovery. While a few still function today, most lie abandoned—memories to a long gone era.

Also among the abandoned buildings in Jermuk is the Palace of Culture. Unfortunately, I arrived too late to witness the busts of renowned Armenian writers, painters, and musicians that once adorned the white, second-storey walls. What remained were empty indoor and outdoor swimming pools, fragments of a once-active library, and the ruins of an 800-seat theatre hall.
