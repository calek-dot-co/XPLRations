Monument to Fallen Defenders

Pleso, Croatia

Ring of oppression.

During the Axis occupation of Zagreb in World War II, thousands of residents within the ring of central Zagreb were executed deported to concentration camps or as part of the persecution of ethnic minorities. Among them was the Roma family Nikolić, commemorated today by a brass cube set into the pathway leading to the Pleso spomenik.

Breaking the ring.

In early May 1945, the Yugoslav Army closed in on Zagreb’s defensive ring, advancing from the south-east against German and Croatian nationalist forces. Months of sabotage by Partisan fighters, along with American bombing raids, had already crippled key railway and airport infrastructure, paving the way for the assault. The collapse of the defensive ring triggered a rapid retreat and surrender of Axis forces. The one-metre offset at the top of this squared-circle spomenik is symbolic of the Partisan breakthrough and subsequent victory.
