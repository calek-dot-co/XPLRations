Costal Shooting Range

Somewhere, Cyprus

Along the rugged coastline of Cyprus lies a rocky parcel of land that appears unremarkable at first glance. That is until you notice that each road approaching this area has a gate, an empty flagpole, and a big sign. On that sign, in English, Greek, and Turkish you can read the following: "Military Firing Range. Keep out when red flags are flying or red lamps are lit."

Used periodically by the British Military for live-fire exercises, the landscape is scarred with rusting Chieftain tanks and dilapidated M113 troop carriers. Through countless bullet holes, you can glimpse concrete-filled interiors, likely filled long ago to prolong the life of these practice targets.

When the military isn't here, it seems the public is free to drive in and walk around. It's just worth remembering the other text on the entrance sign: "Danger. Do not touch any military debris; it may explode and kill you.
