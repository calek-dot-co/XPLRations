Abandoned Hotels of Kupari

Kupari, Croatia

Less than 10km from Dubrovnik, a short bus ride away from tourism, party boats, and the well traveled path is... Kupari: A small bay with a picturesque beach surrounded by abandoned and decaying hotels.

The first hotel was developed in the 1920s when a tile factory owner realised the potential of this bay's tourism, the rest of the hotels followed around the 1960s as the area became popular with Yugoslav military officers and their families. Nearby is even 1 of 34 villas from former Yugoslav President Josip Broz Tito, but that is guarded by a small military booth and seriously worded signs. Maybe next time.

Then during the 1990s the Balkan Wars were not kind to these hotels, attacked and used by various armys until in the 2000s when they were abandoned for good.

Today all hotels in this bay (Grand, Kupari, Goričina, Goričine II, and Pelegrin) are abaondoned. Free for you to wander up off the beach into the husks of concrete, rubble, and overgrowth to explore. Even if you have to climb improvised stairs made from old doors, finding roof access is easy in most hotels and provides the best view of next hotel over.
