Soviet Knitting Factory

Yeghegnadzor, Armenia

Producing children's sweaters, underwear, and notably, gloves and socks for the Soviet military, this former knitting factory was once a big employer in Yeghegnadzor.

But following the dissolution of the Soviet Union, the factory ceased operations in the 1990s. Its machines, materials, and supplies have sat largely preserved for the past thirty years, in part due to the watchful eye of factory owner Hayk.

Storage room

Gas masks, iodine tablets, and emergency instructions share the upstairs storeroom with mechanical sewing hooks, thread, and books on Lenin—remnants of daily life and Cold War-era nuclear preparedness (or perhaps paranoia).

Pattern library

Manager's office

This is Hayk, former manager and current owner of the Yeghegnadzor Knitting Factory. With our limited shared knowledge of the German language, he led me on an enthusiastic tour through every room of the facility, explaining each machine, part, and detail along the way.

Hayk took great pride in the quality of the fabrics and clothing produced here, as evidenced by their widespread export to numerous Soviet Union countries.
