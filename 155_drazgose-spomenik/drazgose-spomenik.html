Cankar Battalion Monument

Dražgoše, Slovenia

This spomenik marks the location where 200 Yugoslav Partisan soldiers from the Cankar Battalion bravely stood against more than 2,000 German troops in battle. Although the Partisans were ultimately defeated and the local villagers punished (despite their scepticism towards and lack of support of the Partisan fighters), this confrontation represented the most significant resistance the Germans had encountered in World War II up to that point.

In the aftermath of the defeat, the village was burnt and blown-up, while many of the remaining residents were deported to concentration camps. Thirty-five years later, this monument was unveiled to the public, commemorating those who sacrificed their lives in the fight for freedom.

Battle of Dražgoše Mosaic

Situated under the overhanging concrete entranceway, this vivid depiction of the 1942 battle at the Mošnje Pasture was created by Ive Šubic, based on his first-hand experiences in the Battle of Dražgoše.

It depicts the snow-covered battlegrounds, local Partisan resistance fighters, Axis troops, oppressed villagers, and the horrors of war.
