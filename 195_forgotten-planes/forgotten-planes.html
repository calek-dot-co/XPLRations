Forgotten Hellenic Aircraft

Somewhere, Greece

As the decades pass and the Hellenic Air Force’s fleet continues to age, many retired aircraft are now visible on satellite maps, often parked at the ends of military airport runways. They sit in haphazard rows close together, sometimes in tall grass, or in messy piles after being stripped for spare parts.

While we await (and hope for) their relocation to less secure areas, a few decommissioned aircraft can be found at civilian airports, which are significantly easier to access. This small, single-runway airport hosts five vintage American planes from the 1960s, three of which I was able to view up close.

Lockheed T-33A Shooting Star
Two seat trainer. Entered service 1951, retired 2000.

Northrop F-5M Freedom Fighter
Single seat fighter/bomber. Entered service 1965, retired 2001.

Lockheed F-104G Starfighter
Single seat fighter/bomber. Entered service 1964, retired 1993.
