Pigeon Poo Church

Somewhere, Greece

In the middle of a fading lignite mine in Greece lies a mini oasis, a green paradise decaying from within as it is reclaimed by nature. In contrast, the surrounding landscape has continued to be tamed by humans. It has been so extensively excavated and reshaped that the church and its small lake now appear to have sunken into the fields.

This is the story of one of several abandoned churches from the former town of K******. With the rest of the town relocated to make way for more open-cut mining, these churches now stands alone, forgotten, and surrounded by overgrowth.

Deserted by people sometime after 2017, this church gets its new name from the decorations of its new occupants. Piles of excrement have built up below pigeon roosts, while walls have been splattered from all directions by busy birds. The few surviving icons of the church are now joined by new companions, lifeless skeletons that stand beside them in silent watch.

Bucket-Wheel Excavator

Not far from the church stands one of the machines likely responsible for reshaping this landscape. Though smaller than massive excavators like Bagger 258, it still looms overhead as you walk around and beneath it.
