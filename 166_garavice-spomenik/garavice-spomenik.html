Garavice Memorial Park of the victims of Fascist Terror

Bihać, Bosnia and Herzegovina

Fifteen columns gather at the peak of Garavice Hill and spread down the hillside on the outskirts of the town Bihać.

Fifteen columns crafted from bihacite stone are layered block by block, with the top block leaning forward, evoking a sorrowful, slumped figure.

Fifteen columns represent grieving women who lost husbands, children, and loved ones to ultra-nationalist Ustaše forces in July of 1941.

Fifteen columns catching sight of the past and looking towards the future stand as a Yugoslav-era monument to the anti-fascist resistance.
