Tito's Bunker, aka Armijska Ratna Komanda D-0

Konjic, Bosnia and Herzegovina

Hidden behind a house, next to a river, near a small town in Bosnia and Herzegovina is one of former Yugoslavia's biggest military secrets. Unlike Željava Air Base, Tito's Bunker survived destruction due to the sabotage of the detonation wires during final evacuation.

The U shaped facility is 280 meters underground, 202 meters long and can withstand a 20-kiloton nuclear blast. Built to protect Yugoslav President Josip Broz Tito and 360 other miliratly leaders, it would have acted as a central military command location in the event of an attack from Soviet Union. With the exception of some questionable art now occupying most rooms, this bunker has been presevered to be very similar to its completed state in 1979.
