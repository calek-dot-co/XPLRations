The Battle of Sutjeska Memorial

Tjentište, Bosnia and Herzegovina

Case Black was an Axis military offensive conducted between 15 May and 16 June 1943. It was the second major attempt to capture the Partisan leader Josip Tito and the rest of the Yugoslav Partisan central command, following the failed Case White operation in January.

Approximately 127,000 troops from Germany, Italy, Croatia, and Bulgaria descended into what is now southern Bosnia and Herzegovina, aiming to encircle the nearly 20,000 Partisan troops and drive them into the Sutjeska valley. As the Axis closed in, the Partisans launched concentrated attacks across the Sutjeska to punch a hole and, for the most part, escaped to Zelengora and eastern Bosnia.

This offensive and the ensuing escape became known as the Battle of Sutjeska. More than 7,000 Partisan soldiers, civilians, and medics were killed, but the survivors—including Tito—altered the course of the war in Yugoslavia. Despite heavy losses, the Partisans preserved their leadership, operational command, and mobile combat units, establishing themselves as the only cohesive, nationwide anti-Axis force. Their survival reinforced morale and strengthened propaganda efforts, positioning the Partisans as a legitimate army capable of enduring—and ultimately winning—the conflict.

The large concrete sculpture shown here serves as a memorial to those who lost their lives in the Valley of Heroes.

Designed by Miodrag Živković and completed in 1971, this spomenik features human faces emerging from an angular, geometric form—also seen in earlier works such as the Monument to Courage, also created by Živković.

"The opening takes the shape of the fighters, very stylized, in an architectonic manner, and simple geometric forms to emphasize the presence of men of the individual fighters in the columns, because it is those columns of men that made the breakthrough at Sutjeska possible."

– Miodrag Živković

Above the spomenik, in a natural hollow of the mountain, is a large amphitheatre. Each tier of its curved seats features the names of the Partisan brigades who fought at the Battle of Sutjeska.

Spomen-Dom, aka. Memorial House

The Spomen-Dom Museum is a memoiral buidling which commemorates the events of the 1943 Battle of Sutjeska via a collection of 13 frescoes and the 7,356 names of the Partisan soldiers who died during the Battle of Sutjeska.

While exploring the outside of the locked building, I spied an open window and used the opportunity to peek at the frescos inside.
