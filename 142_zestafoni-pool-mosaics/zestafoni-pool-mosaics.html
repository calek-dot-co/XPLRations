Pool Mosaics

Zestafoni, Georgia

In the town of Zestafoni, rising above each end of a long dry pool, are six mosaic panels reaching floor to ceiling. Each panel portrays a range of subjects—including mythical creatures, water sports, and people—rendered in the visual language of socialist realism, with stylised, muscular figures in heroic stances.

Recently restored by the Ribirabo Foundation with support from the Polish Solidarity Fund PL in Georgia, these mosaics are in great condition. They will soon be part of the Zestafoni New Industrial Route and Path of Mosaics, a new Zestafoni tourism initative, making them much easier to visit.

Until then, if you wish to visit the pool mosaics before the touristic path opens, speak with the guard in the booth to the right of the main entrance of David Abashidze Stadium. The guard appears to speak only Georgian and Russian, but a small entry fee (bribe) helps to open locked gates and doors.
