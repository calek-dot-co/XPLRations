The City of Parks

Chișinău, Moldova

Upon exiting the airport, you are met by the first of many "welcome" signs found throughout Moldova. The faded red, yellow, and blue stripes mirror the present-day flag, featuring the eagle coat of arms at its centre. For visual context, you can compare this to the Romanian flag and the former Moldova SSR flag.

Location: 46.938471, 28.932441

Architectural brutalism is still standing strong at the Moldtelecom Headquarters. Even through the fall of the Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic and the beginning of the Republic of Moldova, this telecommunications company has remained state-owned in some shape or another.

Location: 47.016949, 28.844690

The Chișinău State Circus once hosted Eastern performances from Belarus, China, Germany, Russia, and Ukraine. After many years of being abandoned, it is now slowly coming back to its former glory thanks to renovation financial support from the European Union and United Nations Development Programme.

Location: 47.037289, 28.849208

The Dance of the Muses mosaic by Alexander Kuzmin from the mid-1960s is slowly crumbling away while awaiting restoration funding for the nearby amphitheatre. Hopefully, not too much more is lost before it's renewed.

Location: 46.999943, 28.854007

My favourite and most unorthodox mosaic from Chișinău, the Plowman of the Cosmos mosaic by Aurel David from 1972. It is made of, what appears to be, irregular broken shards of glass, rather than orderly pieces. It is on the side of one of the I.A. Gagarin Youth Centre buildings, a complex that was apparently illegally privatised and today stands mostly abandoned and decaying. See below for a detail of the glass pieces.

Location: 47.007356, 28.852721

On one side of Stephen the Great Park is the flower fountain mosaic. This enamel mosaic piece was created by Alexandru Kuzmin in 1980. It is unknown if it still functions as a fountain.

Location: 47.026420, 28.828591

The Cascade Stairs, much smaller than Yerevan's Cascade Complex, are still a beautiful sight that leads down to Lake Valea Morilor in Valley of the Mills Park.

Location: 47.021784, 28.816080

On of the Soviet Union's pillars of reform, electrification, is shown in this mosaic by an unknown author. Now partly covered, the text at the top is a quote from Lenin, which says, "Communism is the power of the Soviets, including the electrification of the entire country".

Location: 47.022193, 28.804195

HET! Russian for "no", tops this "No to Thermonuclear Weapons" mosaic. What a shame if the drainpipe cancels out the message.

Location: 47.035604, 28.842457

The Eternity Memorial Complex is a large memorial park in honour of the Soviet soldiers who died in the Great Patriotic War fighting the Nazis (also known as World War II). Opened on May 9, 1975, it was restored on August 24, 2006. The centrepiece of this complex is the 25-metre-tall, five-pointed hollow pyramid with an eternal flame in the centre.

Location: 47.009176, 28.832882

Hotel Național, a closed four-star hotel in Chișinău, is another apparently illegally privatised building that is hard to miss in the centre of Chișinău. Standing on the corner of Constantin Negruzzi Blvd and Strada Ciuflea, it towers over your entrance to the city when entering from the south-east. Over the years, it's had many different paint jobs, from graffiti to the Ukrainian flag, to the current Romanian flag (there is no coat of arms in the middle to make it Moldovan).

Location: 47.014662, 28.848811
