Stucco Collective Farm

Somewhere, Moldova

Despite Moldova being the second smallest of the 15 Socialist Soviet Republics, the party leadership maintained an ambitious vision to transform the nation into the "garden of the USSR." Its fertile, rolling landscape served as a canvas for a broad range of plans spanning everything from strange atomic experiments to industrialised farming collectives.

Situated in a predominantly Bulgarian enclave of Moldova, this farm likely served as the primary employer for the local community in its prime.

Today, the forgotten land is only kept busy with the decaying artworks depicting work and politics on a Молочно-товарная ферма. Roughly translated as a “Dairy-Commodity Farm”, the facility was once a hub for the state’s dairy production.

Work-life on this farm is illustrated in this stucco. From the sun and birds in the sky, to the machines and animals on the ground, to showcasing the produce the entire ecosystem created.

Politics on the farm is best summarised by this image, in what I imagine was a former boardroom for farm management. Its wall-sized painting of the red flag, with what presumably was Lenin's face right in the centre, would have watched over the All-Union workers as they ran this collective dairy farm.
