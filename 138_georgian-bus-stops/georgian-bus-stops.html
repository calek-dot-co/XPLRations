Georgian Bus Stops

1. Tsikhisdziri bus stop

Just outside a small rural town—so small that the nearby riverbed gravel factory dwarfs it—stands a colourful bus stop mural. Using a technique similar to vitreous enamel, the artwork depicts large cats, birds, grapes, the sun, and horseback announcers, capturing the attention of every passerby on the road to Russia.

Location: 42.191776, 44.676580

Translation of the Russian text: "A true friend will help a friend, trouble does not scare him. He will give his heart for a heart, and love is a star on the road".

2. Natakhtari bus stop

Worse for wear compared to the first mural, this bus stop sits on the southern edge of a town along the Georgian Military Highway. Although some panels are missing, it still vividly depicts horse-riding warriors engaged in battle, juxtaposed with a cooking scene featuring Churchkhela: the beloved Georgian sweet made from grape must, nuts, and flour.

Location: 41.916367, 44.726852
